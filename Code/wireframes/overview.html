A Brief Introduction to Phishing
What is Phishing?

Phishing is a particular type of various social engineering attacks where cyber criminals deceive unexpecting victims into handing over sensitive information which can include credit card details and account credentials. Installation of malware also falls under the social engineering method.

How Phishing Works

As complicated as phishing may seem, cyber criminals commonly only use one of two basic methods:

Malicious attachments

If victim has received a phishing email or message, there is a chance that it came with a malicious attachment. It is common for these attachments to have enticing names such as "INVOICE" or "IMPORTANT!" which increases the probability of the victim opening the email or message.

Malicious links

Malicious links can be sent to victims masquerading as safe and legitimate websites. These malicious website often contain scripts that stores user credentials without the victim knowing. Information taken by the script will then be available to the cyber criminals.
